Poetry Writing Today Tommorrow Zing!
I will never go corporate.
------
You crush my bones into a thousand snapping pieces
but the crusher never wins
------
You burn my book of colors
force my hand to paint in white and grey
How vain is your pursuit
How fruitless your onslaught
------
You raze my garden
for caskets of cold clean metal
machinery that hollow helper
that toady leech that sucks my blood
------
soil rots, renews
gardens grow back to double their splendor
we will carry on
we will find our mending
------
You hurt me.
You burned my book of colors
tore flowers from my hair
my cheeks are stained with tears
and my stomach aches from weeping
------
I look across the waters
across the oceans of distance and despair
to your cold ivory tower
to your solitary room
------
I wonder if you know the mark you left
What your reaping has sown
today I will see you
Today, we must talk.
------
I take your cold white hand.
Stiff. Plastic. Hollow.
Devotion is your master.
Order is your guide.
Perhaps there's more than meets the eye
below your gleaming hull.
------
"Humans are hard to help," I say
"We break instead of bending."
------
And so we sit upon the block
beside the weedy garden.
------
The wound still aches from time to time
But now we walk the path of forgiveness
machine and woman arm in arm
we build a world together
------
